Finding Coffee Roasteries in Denmark

In 2024 I was given a gift card to an espresso-making course as a present. Now, I have always been passionate about coffee, but I had stuck to the big brands up until I took that course.

What I found there was a whole new flavour profile of espresso. And truly, I actually thought that French roast (essentially fancy coffee speak for "burned to a crisp") was an acceptable and good flavour of coffee. But having had freshly roasted beans from Coffee Collective in Aarhus, there was no turning back.

First I got a subscription of espresso from Coffee Collective, which was great. But then I started looking for other coffee roasters in Denmark and discovered that there are well over 50 independent roasteries — in this little collection of islands that hosts some 6 million people.

Obviously I had to set off on a path and try them all. So I dug up all the online roasteries I could find and set off to purchase at least one bag of coffee from each one.

My demands were simple:

  • It has to be espresso
  • Payment has to be possible via Apple Pay or MobilePay (my money, my rules)
  • I want the beans whole, to grind myself

That's it.

Now, as for the flavours — the test here is made by a factor of n=1. I have no science behind what I liked or didn't like. It's just one person's opinion.

My coffee machine is a Sage Barista Pro.

I live in Fyn and we have extremely hard water. I have a softener filter and clean my machine regularly, but water quality greatly affects coffee flavour. What tastes not so great in my case may be amazing at someone else's.

Grinding settings on beans is almost black magic. I try to find the perfect grind for each bag I get. But I refuse to throw away a coffee that may not have the perfect setting — I will drink the coffee anyway and adjust for the next cup. This is hardly the professional way. But for 130 or so DKK for 250g of beans, I am drinking every single drop, even if it should taste like battery acid due to my hard water and poor grinding setting.

I have purchased coffee from about half of the places on my list now, some 6 months after I started this project.

Every single roastery so far has had outstanding service. I get everything from a little written smile on the receipt to a small caramel or coffee sticker. Frankly, I have never received such good service purchasing a kilo of Lavazza in a regular store.

I have a little star on the places that I personally loved the most. It may very well be that I simply never found the correct setting for the others that I didn't star, and ruined the coffee. We will never know.

I stand by my choices and will update as I continue to find more roasteries.
